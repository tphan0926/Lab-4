About Me

I am a student interested in Information Systems, with a strong focus on working with databases and organizing data in meaningful ways.

In my IS 436 course, I learned how to design and build databases using concepts like normalization, entity-relationship diagrams, and SQL. I also gained experience with the System Development Life Cycle (SDLC), which helped me understand how to plan, design, and develop systems step by step. Along with databases, I have worked with tools like GitHub, Docker, and Bootstrap to create projects that are both functional and user-friendly.

I enjoy solving problems with data and finding ways to make systems more efficient, and I am continuing to grow my skills through hands-on projects and learning new technologies.
